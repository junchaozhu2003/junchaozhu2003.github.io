Nanchang City Symbols

Visual Identity System / Cultural Communication / Print Design

Project Overview

Nanchang City Symbols is a visual identity project designed to introduce Nanchang, China to international audiences through simplified landmark icons and applied graphic design outcomes. The project explores how city identity can be communicated through accessible visual symbols across print-based formats.

Role

Visual identity design, icon development, layout design, print application, visual research and project documentation.

Tools

Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign and visual research methods.

Focus

Cultural communication, brand consistency, landmark simplification and multi-format visual application.

Research & Concept

The project began with research into Nanchang landmarks, tourism materials and visual identity references. I focused on selecting recognisable landmarks and simplifying them into clear graphic forms that could be understood by audiences without relying heavily on language.

Visual Development

I developed a set of landmark icons by reducing architectural details, testing proportions and refining the visual relationship between different symbols. The aim was to create a consistent identity system rather than separate decorative images.

Colour System

I tested light and dark colour approaches to check how the identity could work across different backgrounds while keeping the landmark symbol recognisable.

Applications

The identity system was applied across postcards, stamps, calendars and tourism communication materials. These outcomes helped test whether the visual language could remain consistent across different print formats.

Reflection

This project helped me understand how visual identity can support cultural communication. It also made me consider the balance between simplifying a city into recognisable symbols and avoiding the oversimplification of its cultural complexity.
